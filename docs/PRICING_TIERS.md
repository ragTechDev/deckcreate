## Pricing Evaluation

### The automation factor

DeckCreate removes the technical labour from every tier. Hobby is essentially: run sync → run transcribe → review → insert intro/outro → export — roughly **35 minutes** of hands-on time. Growth and Professional still carry meaningful creative work (thumbnail design, b-roll curation, custom overlays), but the pipeline eliminates all the grunt work a traditional editor would bill hours for.

This means per-episode time is substantially lower than industry-standard estimates, which changes the pricing calculus: **Hobby does not need to be priced for time — it needs to be priced for accessibility and volume.** Higher tiers are where margin lives.

| Tier | Price | Hands-on time | Implied rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hobby | S$85 | ~35 min | S$146 / h |
| Hobby Plus | S$120–150 | ~50 min | S$144–180 / h |
| Growth | S$320–400 | ~2.5 h | S$128–160 / h |
| Professional | S$700–850 | ~6 h | S$117–142 / h |

All tiers clear S$115/h. Hobby is intentionally low to attract volume and be a genuinely affordable entry point — the effective rate holds because the pipeline makes it fast.

## Positioning Notes

Poddster prices episode editing, short clips, and subtitles as **separate line items**. Their subtitle add-on is S$453 for a manually proofread file — more than our Hobby tier in its entirety, and that is for automated subtitles only. Proofread subtitles are included from Growth upward. DeckCreate produces episode video, clips, carousels, and subtitles from the same pipeline pass, allowing competitive per-episode pricing while delivering more per engagement.

The differentiating pitch: **one recording session becomes a content engine** — a full-length video, branded episode, 3–5 social clips, a carousel, and a subtitle file, without the piecemeal add-on billing.
